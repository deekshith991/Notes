## Current–Charge Relation

$$
i(t) = \frac{dq(t)}{dt}
$$

---

## Charge from Current

$$
q(t) = q(0) + \int i(t)\,dt
$$

More precise form:

$$
q(t) = q(0) + \int_{0}^{t} i(\tau)\,d\tau
$$

---

## Power Rule of Differentiation

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(t^n) = n t^{n-1}
$$

Examples:

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(t^2) = 2t
$$

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(at) = a
$$

---

## Constant Rule

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(C) = 0
$$

---

## Exponential Differentiation

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(e^{at}) = ae^{at}
$$

Examples:

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(e^{-t}) = -e^{-t}
$$

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(e^{-2t}) = -2e^{-2t}
$$

---

## Trigonometric Differentiation

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(\sin(at)) = a\cos(at)
$$

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(\cos(at)) = -a\sin(at)
$$

Example:

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(\sin(120\pi t)) = 120\pi\cos(120\pi t)
$$

---

## Product Rule

$$
\frac{d}{dt}(uv) = u\frac{dv}{dt} + v\frac{du}{dt}
$$

Example form:

$$
\frac{d}{dt}[e^{-4t}\cos(50t)]
$$

---

## Integration Power Rule

$$
\int t^n dt = \frac{t^{n+1}}{n+1} + C
$$

---

## Trigonometric Integrals

$$
\int \cos(at)\,dt = \frac{1}{a}\sin(at) + C
$$

$$
\int \sin(at)\,dt = -\frac{1}{a}\cos(at) + C
$$

---

## Unit Relation

$$
1\,A = 1\,\frac{C}{s}
$$
